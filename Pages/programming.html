Blendo

Winner of the COGS 300 robot tournament.

Working in a team, I programmed a virtual robot to compete in a tournament against AI-controlled robots created by other students.

The Name

In the late 1990s, Adam savage and Jamie Hyneman, best known for thier roles on Mythbusters, built a combat robot, Blendo, for the TV show BattleBots. Blendo was so effective that its design was banned from future competition. We named our robot after Blendo hoping to leave a similar legacy.

The Task

Our robot needed to collect green spheres and return them to its home base. The robot with the most spheres after 2 minutes was deemed the winner.

The robots had the ability to turn, move forward, and shoot lasers at their opponents, causing them to freeze and drop their spheres.

In order to win, we needed to come up with a solution that handled both locomotion and strategy. We were allowed to use machine learning, an explicitly coded algorithm, or a combination of both.

The Solution

After countless hours of training using reinforcement learning, we realized that our problem was too complex for a purely AI solution. In the end, we decided to hard-code all of Blendo's behaviors with a strategy inspired by our initial attempts at machine learning. We observed that the most successful models collected balls until they possessed the majority, then they switched their focus to offense.
