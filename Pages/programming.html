Blendo

Winner of the COGS 300 robot tournament.

Summary

Working in a group, I designed and programmed a virtual robot to compete in a tournament against robots created by my peers.

The Name

In the 1990s, Adam savage and Jamie Hyneman (who you probably know from Mythbusters) built a combat robot named Blendo for the TV show BattleBots. Blendo was so effective that its design was banned from competition. We named our robot after Blendo hoping to leave a similar legacy.

The Task

The robots needed to collect green spheres and return them to their home base. The robot with the most spheres after 2 minutes was the winner.
The robots had the ability to turn, move forward, and shoot lasers to stun their opponents.
We needed a solution that handled both locomotion and strategy. We were allowed to use machine learning, an explicitly coded algorithm, or a combination of both.

The Solution

After experimenting with reinforcement learning, we decided that a purely AI solution was not well suited for this problem. So, we hard-coded all of Blendo's behaviors with a strategy inspired by our best results. The most successful models collected spheres until they gained control over the majority, then they switched their focus to offense.

Lessons Learned

If I could do it all over again, I would use reinforcment learning to handle Blendo's movement and a hard-coded algorithm for strategy.
